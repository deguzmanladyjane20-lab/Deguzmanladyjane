Lesson 1: The Computing Industry

1. Foundations

This section covers the basic definition of computing and its evolution into an industry.

  • Core Definition: Computing is any activity that requires or utilizes computer machinery to achieve a specific goal.
  • Human Impact: Focuses on how technology is used to improve human life.
  • Business Context: An economic sector centered on the research, development, sales, and production of technology.
2. Theory

Focuses on the science behind computers and its application in the entertainment industry.

  • Theory of Computation: The study of what computers can and cannot do using algorithms and symbols.
  • Automata Theory: A branch involving abstract machines, which is essential for developing Artificial Intelligence (AI).
  • Scientific Distinction: Theory is based on evidence and observation, while a Hypothesis is merely a proposal or an educated guess.
  • Multimedia Computing: Designing digital content such as video games, VR/AR, and animation.
  • Rapaport’s Insights: The concept that computers can represent anything using just two objects (0 and 1) through five basic actions.
3. Infrastructure

Covers the practical application of computers in the workplace and the security of data.

  • IT Focus: Developing and maintaining systems, software, and networks.
  • Data Lifecycle: IT professionals are experts in manipulating, storing, retrieving, and transmitting data.
  • Information Ethics: Emphasizes privacy and the ethical treatment of data during storage or broadcasting.
  • The Tech Sector: The collective term for IT companies and professionals.
4. Localization

The state of technology and the available opportunities within the Philippines.

  • Information Economy (IE): Composed of the ICT sector (services and manufacturing) and the Content/Media sector.
  • Growth Trends: Based on PSA (2021) data, there are over 750,000 employees in ICT services, with a significant 133.1% surge in mobile business transactions.
  • Career Paths: High-paying roles include Research Scientists, Network Architects, Security Analysts, Database Administrators, Software Developers, and Web Designers.

References: COMP001 Module 01 Course Lecture; PSA 2021 Survey on ICT (SICT); U.S. BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook (2025).
