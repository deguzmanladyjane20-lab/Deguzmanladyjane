Lesson 6: Electronic Media

1. Platforms

Media serves as the backbone of public awareness, functioning to educate, inform, and entertain.

  • Traditional Media: Established channels such as billboards, flyers, newspapers, radio ads, and television commercials.
  • Electronic Media: Content accessed via digital electronics, including internet material and digital recordings. It has transformed society by removing distance barriers and making global information sharing cheaper.
2. Evolution

The history of electronic media is defined by the move toward wireless communication.

  • Morse Code: Samuel F. Morse created the system of dots and dashes used for emergency distress signals.
  • Wireless Signals: Heinrich Hertz proved radio waves exist, while Guglielmo Marconi pioneered the first wireless devices in 1896.
  • Milestones: Radio evolved from news bulletins in 1916 to a global information powerhouse by 1927, serving as a critical military tool during WWII.
3. Ethics

With the rise of digital content comes the need for protection and integrity.

  • Intellectual Property: Includes Copyright (the exclusive right to publish/sell work) and the prevention of Plagiarism (unauthorized use of ideas).
  • Media Regulation: In the Philippines, the MTRCB regulates material availability and provides age-based advisories for television content.
4. Governance

Laws created to manage the "Digital Divide" and combat digital threats.

  • Cybercrime: Includes Cyberbullying (online humiliation), Phishing (fraudulent data collection), and Digital Piracy (illegal copying).
  • R.A. 10175: Known as the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012, this Philippine law prosecutes illegal access, child pornography, and online libel.
